saturn for his spangled procession 

lucas benitez

Genre

Genre

Genre

Saturn sits upon a boulder, decorated with thorns, flowers, and vinegar. He peers over a thick fog which
looks over a baroque ocean, glistening with some reflection of gleaming light dozing from the distance.
He lifts a garland of thorns and caresses the nape of his neck with it, resting the vicious prickly texture on
his pretty white neck; His son, carrying a basket and resting a chain on his shoulder, silently enters the
scene and patiently stands before his father. Saturn notices him.

Saturn: What delicacies do you bring me?
Folk wood carving, idol figures, scripture of your own?
I could expect it, these things are predictable.
That great chain tugs at you.
why not please me?

Saturn glares at his son’s basket

Saturn: That basket…
It’s vulgar.

Saturn’s son fiddles his thumb over the top of the handle

Saturn’s son: it was woven some eighteen months ago, from some deeply unsettled zone.

An awkward air lightly invades the scene and a creeping sense of dread fills Saturn’s son

Saturn: Do you wash your hair there- Was your life bare living in a place like that.
and when you think of your life am I there?
Go sleep now, and dream on your ‘fulfilling life’; it’ll be the closest you’ll get to it
The trades you brought me are not necessary gifts.
And I am reminded in a sea of pity much like that damned light.

Overblown by this rich oily sea.
why I wished for this moment. Its beyond me.

Saturn turns his body over, in a mechanical inhuman way, pushing the begrudged flowers and thorns
aside and sits overwhelmed with joy. Saturn’s son, expressionless and underwhelmed, stands still and
stares at his father who is enchanted by the glare of the far-off light glistening over the oily dark sea.
Saturn’s son begrudged, steps back in a reluctant movement, stares at his basket, places it on the rocky
ground and exits the scene- his chain cackles per every step until it is faintly present. Saturn begins to
shake lightly and opens his eyes to a great inhumane extent.
Saturn: why, why must this curse be placed upon me.
its poisoning hand delicately cradles my temporal and runs its fingers down to my atlas.
And that light, unattainable yet soothingly close.
The suffering I feel is pure ecstasy, I’m glad in the end. I’m glad.
I wish my procession, that light – spangled sparkled in golden glittered, bright. It is mine.

Saturn in a manic mood begins to violently shake, in pure glee. Saturn stands up in contrapposto looking
out to the light in the distance which has consistently been gleaning Saturn’s attention. The tidal waves
lightly crashing against the tall rocky pulpit; convince Saturn to throw his pretty boneless body into the
sea. Saturn silently glances at the quiet accepting water and throws himself into the sea; that dark oily
green, its invasive spicy odor and the foam that gathers around the rocks- it all accepts Saturn, and he
quickly sank into the bleak ocean.
The scene closes, the tall rock, still dressed with thorns, flowers and vinegar; the basket attentively waits,
the light at the distance unchanged and the sea set quiet. Saturn’s son not even looked back; and a
quaalude to the scene never sent.
