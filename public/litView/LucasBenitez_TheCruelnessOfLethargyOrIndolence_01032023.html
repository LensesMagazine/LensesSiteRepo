The cruelness of lethargy or indolence

A man was cast into a horrible punishment after he was caught disobeying a god’s decree and dressing
too extravagantly.

Ivo: trapped in a glossy cage is oppressively pressed against a cold prideful glass. he is in a stretched and
exposed position, his chest is bare and exposed to the world, his primped breath produces a thick layer
of condensation that obscures his vision. In this state Ivo is so genuine no leper or fool can mistake
themselves as to not notice the gentle raising movement that his breathing causes, his heart gently
beats. In this state Ivo exists trapped inside a glass cage, the glass runs ambiguously tall and gives
enough room for some movement, its tip is rounded off as to make the glass cage describable as a
cylindrical object with a semi-sphere crown. the glass boundary is full of large pieces of paper with
written messages on them. An obscured figure enters the scene, and stands to Ivo’s side, isolated from
Ivo’s presence, just behind the glass.
Ivo, with his iniquitous locks of classical hair and his single pierced right ear, gently flings his abdomen
around slitting it gently against the edges of the paper stacked in his environment in order to assume a
humbler pose. this movement also aided Ivo in avoiding the buildup of condensation against the glass.
His earing is a tear shaped emerald that hangs from a gold chain. It all serves to catch the attention of a
young man pondering Ivo.
It was after Ivo’s jerked movement, that Ivo could clearly see the now clearly defined figure. Ivo’s eyes,
glistening and honest, glance at the figures shoes: they were black and nicely fit, Ivo’s glance ricochets
against the young man’s outfit: simple and dark, unbearably uniform-like; and in one swift movement,
Ivo peeks at the young man’s face where he notes the man’s conventional yet handsome appearance,
his clear and fair skin, his nicely trimmed hair and his piercing unmoving eyes; aided by his apathetic
facial expression.
Ivo, stuck in his cage feels an unbearable weight on his heart, as if the pride of a million resting lions was
gently set on him, by an unaffected hand. Him, as atlas, as the representative of order and sacrifice
quickly glances back up at the young man who is watching him, he attempts to communicate with the
quiet figure.

IVO: why do you glance at my bare body with no such grace? No moron or fool is “brave” enough to
decry the vanity of others.
*Ivo stays still and allows a small amount of time for the young man to respond.
*Ivo attempts to signal to the young man who is patiently watching him and analyzing his locks of hair
and his pitiful situation, with a slight raise of his eyebrow.
IVO: well?...

Ivo in an uncomfortable position, stares at the young man as he patiently watches him; considering the
fleeting time spent in the last few moments. the young man turns his head sideways and begins to
stumble away, the first two steps are stepped backwards and still facing Ivo, the young man stops
himself immediately, turns, facing the glass container while ignoring Ivo and begins walking away past
the glass as he scoffs.
When Ivo watched that unmoved young man begins to walk away he felt an anxious urge to push
through the glass and grasp at his legs and beg him for acknowledgement, upon the realization of his
boundaries, he diverted his attention to his paper filled vessel and peered at the silver-like sharp paper.
as Ivo thought to himself and visualized the leap to the other side of the container, he acknowledged the
pain in watching the young man leave and the pain of the paper cuts. He then closes his eyes delicately
and bravely tosses himself through the paper marking it velvety and crisp, he then gently lays at the
other side pressing his body up at the glass, seeking some comfort, he re-adjusts and finally feels
comfortable; this ‘comfort’ laid way for a buildup of condensation to form. Oppressively: the thick
condensation settles in such a way that turns the young man into a vague figure. Ivo aguishly slides
down against the glass while also flipping over and is finally able to read one of the notes “don’t let the
Perrier get to your head”; a sudden realization sets in: Ivo’s eyes widen, until the moment is interrupted
by the sound of steps. Ivo can barely make out that they’re growing louder.
Ivo’s fate was sealed for him before he ever knew what he was.
