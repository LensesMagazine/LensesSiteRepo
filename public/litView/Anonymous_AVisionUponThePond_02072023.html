A Vision Upon the Pond 

Anonymous

Genre

Genre

Genre

I look at myself in a mirror,
I look at myself in a pond.
I breathe the air of the past,
And of knowledge I am fond.

I’ll be stalked by someone whom I may know
I’ll be hurried by someone whom I fear,
I’ll be persecuted by my memories,
And soon their penitent steps I’ll hear

I’ll walk in the desert by day,
I’ll walk in the desert by night,
I’ll reach up a valley with a dark fall,
And then peer into a dimly lit sight.

I’ll go back to where I came from
I’ll stare on shining eyes in the plains,
I’ll look at them and they’ll look at me back
And I’ll feel blood pumping through my veins.

I’ll shed tears from eyes that do not blink,
I’ll feel tremors through bodies that do not move,
I’ll feel dangers that could possibly be real,
And see what logic cannot prove.

I’ll turn around and hear the wind now whistle,
I’ll see them get a bit more close,
I’ll stop and turn to the eyes just once again,
And find that the wind no longer blows.

I’ll see how their eyes glow,
I’ll see their bizarre form,
I’ll see their thousand stares,
And how they gaze at me with scorn.

I’ll doubt of what to do,
I’ll try to end it quick.
I’ll admit there isn’t hope,
But still do something slick.

I’ll go back to the valley,
I’ll try to make my run,
I’ll try to end it all,
And suffering there’ll be none.

I’ll draw my knife as well,
I’ll admit I will be shaken.
I’ll try to stab myself
And die before I’m taken.

I’ll get near to the fall,
I’ll get close to the end,
I’ll make my final jump,
And to fate my spirit send.

I’ll get dragged by my feet,
I’ll feel my knife now slip,
I’ll be seized from my blade,
And in the darkness will dip.

I’ll be turned back again,
I’ll feel cold upon my face,
I’ll watch a cluster form,
And then my heart will race.

I’ll gasp and cry a lot,
I’ll feel something within,
I’ll try to kick my feet,
And feel burning in my skin.

I’ll feel how the cluster comes,
And whispers to my ear,
‘‘Your body now is mine,’’
‘’Your end is very near.’’

I move away from the pond,
I squirm with anguish and fear
I hold my chest with both my hands,
And I know the night is near.
