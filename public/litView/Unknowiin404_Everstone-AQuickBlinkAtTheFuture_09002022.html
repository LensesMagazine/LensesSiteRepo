Everstone - A Quick Blink at The Future 

Unknowiin404

Genre

Genre

Genre


     There was no question that there was a shoe in the middle road. Pass-byers and townsfolk simply stared at it as they strolled on by, minding their own business. It was then when a small boy got close to it and disappeared afterwards, that the public instantly became worried. Where had it come from, some would finally cry. Well allow me to answer, and mark my words, for the wretched footwear was cursed, dying of starvation.
     A vigilante none other than a widely known one at that, had been reconsidering his role in society after staining his hands for far too long. His benign sidekick hadn’t the slightest clue of this bloody side of his, and believed that what they were doing was for the good of the people.
     That was, until his love interest vanished, not a trace left behind–not even her emerald rings. Ray’s sudden change in personality must have given it away, for the man was the sudden accused in an instant, both men taking to the skies, caught in a forlorn battle: one using weaponry, and the other using alchemy.
     Bandit at last decided that enough was enough.
     He casted a curse as a last resort on his once dear idol, using a spell many of those studying such great power would never dare to cast on a single soul, for it was a spell not even the coldest of hearts could ever live through.
     “Apologies, old friend,” he’d murmur through salty tears, tossing the golden footwear far from his peripheral vision, a distance that he’d assumed was too far for anyone to have close contact with.
     But oh how wrong our hero had been, as the shoe lost its glimmer, tainted with bile and crimson that were the marks of innocent wildlife and pedestrians, deaths too unnecessary to have happened. Each day he would grow hungrier, vigilante no more, a taste for revenge on his “beloved” friend who’d stabbed him in the back like so.
     And thus, dear comrades, is why I must warn you, that the rose-gold shoe is not to be tampered with, as Ray had now taken his first victim in decades, growing stronger with each life he takes from anyone. From the most foolish of men and women, to the slyest roadkill that he would watch die at the wheels of automobiles in satisfaction.
