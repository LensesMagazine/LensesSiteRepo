Stained Glass... 

CXPHER

Poem

Featured Piece

Tag 3

"My inspiration behind this piece was someone I grew close to. To me, they’re a very colorful and bright person, same way stained glass is. I’ve also seen them in states of vulnerability, a state as fragile as glass. Through our experiences, I also saw how something they were going through would cause them to “break,” and hurt me. I compared that to the way a part of glass could break and leave a shard, something sharp that could hurt somebody. Yet, it’s not just any glass shard; it’s one that belongs to stained glass. I was getting hurt by someone, but it was that person specifically. I hold a soft spot for them. I compare them to stained glass in the poems as a way of saying that they’re worthy and special, even at times when they’re hurting me."

Cubicle Box - Checkers 

Lucas Benitez

Letters

Experimental

Tag 3

"Its very blatant social commentary: the only notable part of the story is the absence of it specifically how the entire story happens. the unnamed main character is a deeply unseen subject Moreso characterized as average than any other trait. The main character sends two deeply emotional “love letters” to himself; it isn’t specifically narcissism, or self-love either if anything it is a crescendo of self-loathing. I reference some type of sociology as the main character is a increasingly depressed lost person who doesn’t know exactly what he wants or even knows, his reality is swept off its feet and in an attempt to cope he turns inwards with hatred and becomes an increasingly aggravated person. really its a peer into the most average person to exist; and i adapted my writing style to fit a average person who is educated and most likely middleclass (the reason for some minor untended grammatical errors)"
